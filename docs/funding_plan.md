## Executive Summary

This document prioritizes funding sources to sustain Animetor Eval as a labor-first research + startup project through 2027–2028. The project has three revenue/support pathways: **Publication** (59%), **Policy** (32%), and **Business** (9%), per STANCE.md §2.

**Recommended priority tier sequence**:

1. **Tier 1 (Primary)**: JSPS DC/PD funding (social scientist doctorate + affiliation leverage)
2. **Tier 2 (Secondary)**: Public research grants (JSPS Kakenhi / 文化庁 / 厚労省) + private foundations
3. **Tier 3 (Medium-term)**: B2C SaaS self-bootstrap (post-MVP, documentation-driven)
4. **Tier 4 (Conditional)**: Venture capital (labor-first covenant required)

**Timeline**: JSPS applications cycle Q2 2026 (DC closing) → Q4 2026–Q1 2027 for results. Concurrent outreach to Tier 2 foundations (rolling basis). B2C bootstrapping begins Q3 2026 post-paper-draft.

**Program**: Grant-in-Aid for Scientific Research (Japan-wide competitive research grant)

| Criterion | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Amount range** | ¥1,000,000–¥15,000,000 depending on category (早期採用者 young researcher: 1–3M typical) |
| **Duration** | 2–5 years |
| **Eligibility** | Requires institutional affiliation (host research institution); usually PD or faculty position |
| **Application cycle** | Varies by category; most common: April public notice → July submission → March decision |
| **Commitment** | Detailed research plan + expected outputs (publishable results, data products) |
| **Advantages** | High prestige; substantial budget; supports full research operations (travel, equipment, RA salary) |
| **Limitations** | Affiliation-dependent (cannot apply as independent startup); review is competitive & rigorous; policy requirement is research dissemination (publications / data / symposium), not direct commercialization |

**Feasibility**: Depends on institutional home. If Animetor Eval is integrated into university research program (policy research track per STANCE.md §4.5), Kakenhi is viable. If operating as pure startup, ineligible.

**MUST ASK USER**: Is there an institutional research home (university, research institute, NGO) that would host this project for funding purposes, or is it operating as independent startup entity?

## Tier 3: B2C SaaS Self-Bootstrap (MEDIUM-TERM)

### Strategy

**Timeline**: Begin Q4 2026–Q1 2027 (post-paper-draft). Explicit funding is **not** a goal here; rather, **low-burn revenue sustainability**.

**Model** (per `docs/b2c_saas_design.md`):

- **Freemium**: Individual animator portfolio search + basic credit aggregation (free tier)
- **Premium (¥500–1,500/month)**: Advanced network analysis, salary comparison benchmarks, studio reputation data
- **B2B (studio HR tools)**: Team analytics, talent mapping, retention prediction (¥5,000–50,000/month by studio size)

**Funding requirement**: Minimal. After MVP (200 free users, <10 paid), marginal cost is API/hosting. **Self-sustainable at 50+ paid users** at ¥1,000 average.

### Tier 3 Financial Runway

| Phase | Timeline | Funding need | Source |
|-------|----------|---|--------|
| **MVP** (search + basic viz) | Q4 2026–Q1 2027 | ¥1–2M | Existing income (JSPS DC/PD or employment) |
| **Pilot (100 free + 10 paid users)** | Q1–Q2 2027 | ¥0.5M (modest marketing) | SaaS revenue overlap |
| **Scaling (500+ free, 50+ paid)** | Q2–Q3 2027 onward | Self-sustaining (gross margin >60%) | Subscription revenue + Anthropic Startup credit (if applicable) |

**Explicit funding NOT needed** for B2C if Tier 1–2 provides runway (¥1–3M/year). B2C is **revenue acceleration**, not funding necessity.

## Tier 4: Venture Capital (CONDITIONAL, LAST RESORT)

### Strategy & Constraints

VC is **not recommended** as primary funding path (STANCE.md §7.2, labor-first risk). Explore only if:

1. JSPS/Kakenhi/foundations all rejected, AND
2. **Non-negotiable labor-first covenants** can be locked into term sheet

### Required Term Sheet Conditions (if VC proceeds)

**Non-waivable clauses**:

1. **Labor-first governance lock**: Board composition ≥50% independent/worker-aligned directors (exclude consumer-facing VCs, exclude PE firms focused on hiring/talent cost reduction)
2. **Mission lock**: Explicit clause prohibiting use of company data for labor-cost optimization or union-busting services
3. **User data sovereignty**: No sale of individual-level person data; aggregated/anonymized research-use only
4. **IP assignment hybrid**: User portfolio data remains user IP; analytical/ER/network code is joint IP; Animetor retains right to publish research regardless of commercial performance
5. **Exit optionality**: Acquirer must accept labor-first mission lock (strategic sale only; no PE acquisition without board veto)

### Venture Capital Sources (if pursuing)

| Type | Examples | Pros | Cons |
|------|----------|------|------|
| **Impact investors** | Astrum, Sampo Capital, Social Venture Funds | Mission-alignment possible | Smaller check sizes (¥10–100M typical) |
| **Tech/data VCs (with labor-policy interest)** | SoftBank Vision Fund (unlikely but comprehensive mandate), Rakuten Ventures | Large check sizes | Less labor-specific alignment |
| **Japanese family offices** | Toyota Ventures, Panasonic Corporate Ventures | Patient capital, mission flexibility | Hidden decision-making; due diligence slower |

**Assessment**: Impact/SV funding is **far more likely** to accept labor-first covenant than traditional tech VC. Approach impact-focused lead only.

## Forbidden Vocabulary Reminder

Per CLAUDE.md & STANCE.md §1.3: **DO NOT use** in funding proposals:

- ❌ "ability", "skill", "talent", "competence", "capacity", "quality"
- ❌ Japanese equivalents: 能力、実力、優秀、劣る、上手い
- ❌ "high-quality" personnel descriptors
- ❌ "strong" / "weak" animators or creators

**Instead use**:
- ✅ "structural position in collaborative networks"
- ✅ "collaboration density", "network centrality"
- ✅ "opportunity structure", "credit visibility patterns"
- ✅ "contribution attribution" (not "output quality")

**Impact on proposals**: Kakenhi / SciREX / 厚労省 reviewers may be unfamiliar with labor-first framing. Frame proposals as **structural observation** (network position, labor-market transparency) rather than **merit evaluation** (ranking creators by quality).

## Key Dependencies & Risks

### Dependency 1: Legal Entity Form (`TASK_CARDS/31_business/01_startup_form`)

**Blocker**: Cannot finalize application strategies until startup form (LLC, general nonprofit, research institute affiliation, etc.) is decided.

**Mitigation**: Finalize by end of Q2 2026; document in TASK_CARD completion.

---

### Dependency 2: Publication Strategy Finalization (`docs/papers/strategy.md`)

**Blocker**: Kakenhi/SciREX proposals must cite expected publications. If strategy shifts (e.g., information-first instead of economics-first), proposal narrative changes.

**Mitigation**: Finalize publication strategy by Q1 2026; reference in all grant proposals.

---

### Risk 1: JSPS DC Rejection (Low probability if eligible)

**If rejected**: PD pathway remains available post-degree (spring 2027 application). Fall back to Tier 2 (foundations + Kakenhi).

**Mitigation**: Begin Tier 2 applications in parallel (June 2026).

---

### Risk 2: Kakenhi / Institutional Tie-in Required

**Issue**: Kakenhi/SciREX eligibility depends on institutional home. If startup operates independently, these pathways are closed.

**Mitigation**: Explore research institute affiliation or university research center partnership by Q2 2026 (separate from legal startup entity).

---

### Risk 3: Foundation Grants Fatigue

**Issue**: Foundation applications are time-intensive (1–2 weeks per proposal). Pursuing 5+ in parallel may dilute quality.

**Mitigation**: Target 3 foundations max in Q3 2026 first wave; assess success rate; iterate.

---

### Risk 4: B2C Revenue Slower Than Forecast

**Issue**: MVP may take longer to develop (underestimated complexity); paid-user acquisition slower than 20 users by Q3 2027.

**Mitigation**: Secure 12–18 month Tier 1/2 funding runway to depressurize B2C timeline. Allow MVP to mature to product-market fit (Q3 2027–Q1 2028 more realistic).
