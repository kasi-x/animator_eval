## Media Relations Philosophy

Animetor Eval operates under **reactive, labor-first principles**. We do not:

- Pursue media coverage or issue unsolicited press releases
- Sell exclusive rights or embargo periods for major findings
- Coordinate timing with industry actors (studios, committees, platforms)
- Provide advance briefings to business-focused outlets in exchange for coverage

We do:

- Respond promptly and factually to all legitimate inquiries
- Separate findings (facts) from interpretation (author position) clearly in responses
- Offer on-call researchers for follow-up, methodology questions, error correction
- Share supplementary materials (data summaries, figures, method notes) for verification

## Interview Guidelines

### Before the Interview

**What we will provide:**
- Fact sheet (one-pager with key definitions)
- Main findings summary (1–2 pages, structured Findings → Method → Interpretation)
- Suggested discussion topics and framing
- Researcher background / academic position

**What to prepare:**
- Specific questions in advance (preferred, but not required)
- Publication date / expected readership (helps us calibrate depth)
- Intended use of quotes (academic vs. popular media; changes tone)

### During the Interview

**Interview formats we support:**
- Voice call (15–30 min, recorded or not)
- Email Q&A (written exchange, good for technical accuracy)
- Written statement (we draft, you confirm before use)
- On-record/off-record hybrid (specify which claims are quotable)

**What we commit to:**
- Answering questions accurately within our expertise (network analysis, labor structures, methodology)
- Acknowledging limits ("I don't know" rather than speculation)
- Distinguishing findings from interpretation ("Here's what the data shows; here's how I interpret it")
- Correcting mis-steps immediately if spotted during conversation

**What we ask of you:**
- No leading questions designed to extract "personal capability assessment" framing
- If accuracy is critical (numbers, methodology), verify quotes before publication
- If we say something off-topic or speculative, you'll confirm it's on-record before using it

### After the Interview

**Publication timeline:**
- We ask for a draft link or excerpt 48 hours before publication (for factual verification, not editorial control)
- We will not request story kills or edits, only flag factual errors

**Response to published coverage:**
- We may publicly post a correction notice if significant factual errors appear
- We will not dispute interpretation or editorial framing (fair game)
- We accept that different outlets will emphasize different findings

## Handling Sensitive Topics

### Gender & Labor Market Inequity

**Our position:**
- We observe structural disparities in credited roles, project frequency, and collaboration patterns
- We do not claim these differences arise solely from discrimination (understanding causation requires more evidence)
- We do not rank genders by personal capability or inherent "worth"

**Framing we support:**
- "Women are underrepresented in high-connectivity roles (bridges, frequent collaborators)"
- "Female animators show different career arc patterns (slower growth in first 5 years)"
- "Controlling for experience and role, women's project frequency XX% vs. male peers"

**Framing we will correct:**
- "Women animators lack skill" (we measure network structure, not capability)
- "Women leave animation more often" (we measure visibility loss, not actual departure)
- "Women have poor networking skills" (we measure observed opportunities, not interpersonal effectiveness)

### Studio-Specific Claims

**Our position:**
- We analyze structural data, not judge studios individually
- Studio performance depends on data quality, project sample, and time period
- We do not target studios in media outreach

**How we respond:**
- If asked "Is Studio X bad?" → "Our data shows Studio X has YY% credit concentration and ZZ% colleague retention. These are facts, not judgments."
- If asked "Is Studio X associated with problem Z?" → "Correlation in our data; determining root factors requires evidence beyond correlation"
- If Studio X criticizes our analysis → We welcome their data/methodology critique; editorial disputes we don't comment on

### Individual Person Identification

**Our policy:**
- We do not highlight individual animators in media outreach (privacy first)
- If journalists identify someone themselves, we confirm structural data but do not add interpretation
- We do not "break news" about individual people

## Data & Methodology Questions

**If journalists ask about:**

- **Scoring methodology**: Refer to `docs/CALCULATION_COMPENDIUM.md` (technical), or email for a 1-page summary
- **Data sources**: One-pager available; sources listed in every report's "Data Statement"
- **Confidence & uncertainty**: Our standard is confidence intervals for person-level scores, null model comparisons for group claims, holdout validation for predictions
- **Potential bias**: We acknowledge limitations in our FAQ and all reports; happy to discuss known biases
- **Reproducibility**: Peer reviewers can request snapshots; public code/data release planned after publication

**For fact-checking:**
- Numbers in our reports come with 95% confidence intervals — quote the range, not just the point estimate
- Sample sizes are provided; small-n findings are explicitly noted
- Time periods and cohort definitions matter — include them in quotes

**What we won't do:**
- Provide unpublished results for exclusive coverage
- Speculate on causation beyond what the data shows
- Rank people or studios in media (that defeats our labor-first purpose)

## Corrections & Retractions

**If we make an error:**

1. **You discover it**: Email us immediately with evidence. We will:
   - Investigate within 3 business days
   - Publish a correction notice on our website if confirmed
   - Offer you the corrected language for a follow-up story

2. **We discover it**: We will:
   - Issue a notice on our website with the error and correction
   - Email affected media outlets proactively
   - Update all publicly available reports (with a changelog note)

3. **What we will NOT do**:
   - Delete or hide past reports (archived versions kept for reproducibility)
   - Retroactively claim a whole study was flawed (errors are specific)
   - Blame your publication for amplifying a mistake we made

## Partnering with Animetor Eval

### We Are Open to:

- **Collaborative reporting**: Working with journalists to investigate specific structural questions (e.g., "How do women's career arcs differ?" → we provide data + you do fieldwork/interviews)
- **Educational media**: Explainers about how network analysis works, what our data can/cannot show
- **Public health of the industry**: Stories about transparency, labor rights, or policy implications

### We Are NOT Open to:

- **Sponsored content**: Anything framed as PR or endorsement
- **Exclusives with commercial strings**: (e.g., "We get first access to your data if we cover it favorably")
- **Embedding in studio promotions**: Not a tool to market individual studios or anime

## When You Are Writing About Us

### Please Include:

- **Labor-first framing**: "Animetor Eval is a research project designed to support worker transparency, not studio hiring"
- **What we're not**: "This is not a competitive ordering system or hiring mechanism; it measures network position and collaboration patterns"
- **Uncertainty**: "Findings are reported with confidence intervals; methodology note available in reports"
- **Opt-out availability**: "Individuals can request removal from analyses"

### Example Framing:

> *Animetor Eval, a research project analyzing anime industry labor through public credit data, found that XX% of production work is concentrated among YY% of collaborators (95% CI: [AA, BB]). The project emphasizes structural observation over personal capability assessment, and individual scores include confidence intervals to reflect uncertainty.*

### Please Do NOT Include:

- "Competitive rank of animators" (we don't rank; we measure network position)
- "AI grades anime workers" (we use publicly available data, not AI for capability scoring)
- "Exposes underperforming studios" (we don't judge studios; we describe structures)
- Unnamed quotes or speculation about motivation
- Our findings as a hiring guide for studios
