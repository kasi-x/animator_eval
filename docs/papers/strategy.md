# Publication Strategy: Economics + Information Science Dual Track

**Document version**: 1.0  
**Last updated**: 2026-05-13  
**Author's standing**: Social scientist + doctoral candidate; independent project (not thesis-bound; faculty advisor not involved)  
**Status**: Final recommendation for adoption

---

## Executive Summary

This memo recommends **Option (b): Economics-primary, Information-secondary** publication strategy. The primary track targets Labour Economics / RIETI / Journal of Cultural Economics (publication weight: 59% of project impact). The secondary track uses NetSci / WebSci workshops and Applied Network Science journal as secondary dissemination channels (estimated timeline offset: Q1 lag).

**Key decision factors**:
1. Labor-first positioning (STANCE.md §1) is most directly served by economics venue publication
2. Economics publication carries explicit policy-relevance pathway
3. Information-science contribution can be extracted from the same core analysis and deployed efficiently as workshop/poster content
4. Single monolithic paper per venue is unnecessarily expensive in author time given data and method overlap

**Recommended timeline**: RIETI DP Q4 2026 → Labour Economics Q2 2027 → NetSci workshop Q1 2027 (parallel track).

---

## Strategy Comparison: Four Options

### Option (a): Complete Separation

**Description**: Entirely separate papers for economics venue and information/network science venue. Distinct research framing, method gates, and result sets.

| Dimension | Evaluation |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Highest: double literature review, separate method gates, distinct data stories |
| **Risk** | Lowest: each paper optimized for its own reviewer expectations; no self-citation tension |
| **Venue fit** | Best: Labour Economics paper ignores network metrics entirely; NetSci paper is methodologically pure |
| **Timeline** | Slowest: serial submission needed (economics venue takes 8–12 months after revision) |
| **Output type** | 2 peer-reviewed articles, completely independent narratives |
| **Remaining-year requirement** | D1–D2 (2+ years minimum) |

**Pros**:
- Zero self-citation / overlap risk
- Each paper maximizes venue-specific rigor expectations
- Cleaner disciplinary positioning

**Cons**:
- Double analysis work (literature, method, revision)
- Same underlying data set analyzed twice delays publication velocity
- Less time for policy impact pathways

**Recommended for**: Researcher with strong prior publications in both fields; minimal time pressure.

---

### Option (b): Economics-Primary, Information-Secondary ← **RECOMMENDED**

**Description**: Lead submission to Labour Economics or Journal of Cultural Economics. Deploy network findings as workshop paper / poster at NetSci or WebSci; possible extended journal version in Applied Network Science (12–18 months post-economics publication).

| Dimension | Evaluation |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Moderate: one core paper fully written; network analysis extracted as secondary publication |
| **Risk** | Moderate: self-citation acceptable in secondary venue; workshop reviewers have lower rejection bar |
| **Venue fit** | Aligned with labor-first stance (STANCE.md §1.1–1.3) |
| **Timeline** | Medium: lead paper 8–12 months; secondary track parallel or sequential without blocking |
| **Output type** | 1 primary peer-reviewed article; 1–2 secondary workshop/journal publications |
| **Remaining-year requirement** | D2–D3 (1.5+ years); acceptable even with higher pressure |

**Pros**:
- Establishes economics reputation first (policy amplification vector)
- Information venue viewed as supplementary, not competing narrative
- Efficient use of shared data and preprocessing
- Workshop submission < 4 months to first decision

**Cons**:
- Information-science contribution may be perceived as lower tier
- Secondary venues have lower citation accumulation
- Still requires self-citation acknowledgment in secondary paper

**Recommended for**: Researcher with doctorate orientation in economics; needs policy publication first; has adequate remaining time for follow-up.

---

### Option (c): Information-Primary, Economics-Secondary

**Description**: Lead submission to NetSci / WebSci / Applied Network Science. Economics findings positioned as secondary venue (labour studies journal / workshop) or policy brief pathway.

| Dimension | Evaluation |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Moderate: one core paper; economics angle secondary |
| **Risk** | Moderate: acceptance velocity faster in information venues; economics implications may read as weaker |
| **Venue fit** | Information-science focused, not aligned with labor-first prioritization |
| **Timeline** | Fastest: NetSci has 4–6 month decision timeline |
| **Output type** | 1 primary peer-reviewed; 1 secondary workshop/policy channel |
| **Remaining-year requirement** | D2–D3 (1+ years), even tighter time window than (b) |

**Pros**:
- Fastest time to first acceptance
- Information-venue publication velocity higher than economics
- Network science inherently publishable without economics framing

**Cons**:
- Labor-first stance deprioritized (STANCE.md §2, publication weight 59% on economics pathway)
- Economics contribution appears secondary → policy pathway weakened
- Economics venue reviewers may ask why labour analysis is not primary

**Recommended for**: Information scientist who happens to work with labor data; economics component understood as illustrative.

**Incompatible with this project**: See STANCE.md §1 and §5.2.

---

### Option (d): Same Dataset, Two Distinct Cuts

**Description**: Single data snapshot yields two separate manuscripts with different outcome variables, cohorts, or time windows. Example: Economics paper on AKM fixed effects with production-scale outcome; information paper on betweenness centrality predicting role progression with different cohort definition.

| Dimension | Evaluation |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Highest: requires dual method gates, separate causal assumptions, distinct data stories |
| **Risk** | **Highest**: Reviewer concern about self-citation, data reuse, non-independence of analysis |
| **Venue fit** | Divergent: forces two separate framing narratives from same source |
| **Timeline** | Medium: can be parallel, but data-independent claim is hard to defend |
| **Output type** | 2 peer-reviewed articles with distinct research questions |
| **Remaining-year requirement** | D1–D2 (2+ years); needs defense against self-citation charges |

**Pros**:
- Extractive efficiency (2 papers from 1 data preprocessing investment)
- Can publish in parallel

**Cons**:
- **Reviewer inevitably asks**: "Why are these two papers not integrated?"
- Self-citation volume is high (same data, same preprocessing pipeline, same entity resolution)
- If reviewer spots overlap in outcome-variable construction, may downgrade as "non-independent"
- Requires explicit, documented explanation of **why outcomes are not derivable from each other**

**Not recommended for this project** (STANCE.md §5.2 does not default to this). Acceptable only if user determines:
1. Economics and information papers answer genuinely distinct research questions
2. Outcome variables are constructively independent (not derived from each other)
3. Both submissions can include "same data, independent research questions" in cover letter

## Recommendation: Option (b) Economics-Primary, Information-Secondary

### Why Option (b) Aligns with Project Stance

**STANCE.md alignment** (§1–2):
- **Labor-first (§1.1)**: Economics publication pathway directly targets policy-relevance venues with explicit labor-market implications
- **Three-track weighting (§2)**: Publication receives 59% weight; economics main track ensures principal policy amplification channel is primary venue
- **Venue hierarchy**: Labour Economics → policy ministry relevance; NetSci workshop → research community relevance; both serve project legitimacy, but economics is load-bearing

**Method-gate rationale**:
- Single core analysis (AKM + graph metrics + statistical inference) can be credibly deployed with:
  - **Economics frame**: Production-scale outcome, studio fixed effects, compensation-basis CI
  - **Information frame**: Centrality measures, collaboration density, community detection
- Information publication is not diminished by secondariness; it is a **genuinely separate research question** that happens to use overlapping data and preprocessing

**Timeline efficiency**:
- Economics paper draft: 4–5 months (Q3–Q4 2026)
- RIETI DP: 6–8 weeks from submission (pre-review, quick turnaround)
- Labour Economics: 8–12 months after revision (Q2 2027)
- NetSci workshop: parallel track, Q1 2027 submission (4–6 week review)
- Applied Network Science extended: follow-up, Q3 2027+

This staggering allows publication velocity without waiting for economics review completion.

## Stop-If Conditions

**Proceed with Option (b) unless**:

1. **Gender distribution (dataset)**: If analysable persons with complete gender/demographic coding < 50% of total sample
   - **Action**: Revert to **economics-only track** with strict method gate: AKM + OLS residual analysis only (no demographic subgroup claims)
   - **Rationale**: Information-track centrality measures on <50% coded population invites demographic-bias criticism; better to use economics paper for structural findings and publish demographic gap analysis separately once data improves

2. **Prior publications**: If user already has ≥2 peer-reviewed publications in either economics OR information science
   - **Action**: Flip primary/secondary to field with weaker record
   - **Rationale**: Publication portfolio diversification

3. **Remaining timeline**: If user's remaining doctoral time < 12 months
   - **Action**: Revert to **workshop-only + preprint strategy** (NetSci workshop + arXiv); defer journal submission to postdoc
   - **Rationale**: Labour Economics alone will take >12 months; risk missing degree deadline

4. **Data resolution failure**: If entity resolution (persons, studios, anime) reaches canonical match rate < 92%
   - **Action**: Delay both tracks pending data quality recovery; publish RIETI DP as **negative result report** on data quality
   - **Rationale**: Low ER rate invalidates both economic and network claims; honesty-first approach

## Open Questions Requiring User Decision

These items cannot be pre-determined and require user judgment:

### Q1: Final venue choice for economics primary
- **Options**: RIETI DP → Labour Economics (recommended) vs. RIETI DP → Journal of Cultural Economics vs. skip DP, go direct to Labour Economics
- **Trade-off**: DP adds prestige + working paper citation, but adds 6 weeks. Direct submission faster but loses DP intermediary audience.
- **User decision**: Prioritize speed or working-paper legacy? (Recommend: do DP; 6 weeks is worth policy intermediary audience.)

### Q2: Gender analysis scope
- **Decision**: Will the paper include gender analysis of network position, or defer as separate future work?
- **Impact**: If included, requires gender-coding ≥80% of person corpus. If excluded, unblocks publication but leaves gendered opportunity analysis for follow-up.
- **User decision**: Scope commitment? (Recommend: defer detailed gender analysis to follow-up paper; mention in policy implications as future work.)

### Q3: Replication snapshot and supplementary materials
- **Decision**: Will we freeze data/code snapshot for Zenodo + DOI at submission (STANCE.md §5.4 exception)?
- **Timeline impact**: +2 weeks for freezing, versioning, README documentation
- **User decision**: High-venue submission warrants snapshot, or acceptable risk to skip? (Recommend: do snapshot; Labour Economics is high-visibility venue.)

### Q4: Extended workshop-to-journal pathway
- **Decision**: If NetSci workshop paper is accepted, do we plan to extend it for Applied Network Science journal, or leave as workshop-only contribution?
- **Cost**: +6–8 weeks writing; +6–9 months review; modest incremental citation
- **User decision**: Extended network contribution valuable, or sufficient as workshop + preprint? (Recommend: commit to extended version; network-science legacy deserves journal treatment.)

### Q5: Relationship with Japan-focused venues
- **Decision**: Should we consider Japan-domestic venues (e.g., 日本労働研究雑誌, 日本経営学会誌) as primary instead of international track, or parallel track?
- **Impact**: Different reviewer expectations (more contextual, less methodologically formal); visibility within METI/文化庁 policy machinery
- **User decision**: International first (recommended, higher citation impact) or Japan-first? (Recommend: international primary; Japan-domestic can be secondary/preprint channel.)

## Forbidden Vocabulary Reminder (Integration Check)

Per REPORT_PHILOSOPHY.md §7 and CLAUDE.md §12:

- ❌ "ability", "skill", "talent", "competence", "capability" (Japanese: 能力、実力、優秀、劣る)

**Implication for paper drafting**:
- Economics paper: "AKM fixed effect" measures structural position, not individual capacity
- Information paper: centrality metrics measure network position, not individual importance
- Both papers: opportunity structures and constraints, not hidden quality differences
